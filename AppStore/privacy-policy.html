Privacy Policy

Ledger Lite  ·  Inkbar  ·  Last updated: May 28, 2026

Ledger Lite is a personal expense tracker built with your privacy as the default. This policy explains what data the app handles and how.

Data we collect

None. Ledger Lite does not collect, transmit, or share any personal data. Everything you enter — expenses, categories, subscriptions, notes — is stored exclusively on your device using Apple's SwiftData framework.

Data stored on your device

The app stores your financial records, preferences, and settings locally on your iPhone. This data never leaves your device unless you explicitly back it up via iCloud (controlled entirely by your Apple ID settings, not by us).

Analytics and tracking

Ledger Lite contains no analytics SDKs, advertising SDKs, or tracking of any kind. We do not use Firebase, Mixpanel, Amplitude, or any similar service.

Third-party services

The app uses the following Apple system APIs:

  • UserDefaults — stores your preferences (home currency, biometric lock setting, onboarding state) on-device.
  • Face ID / Touch ID — used only to unlock the app locally. Biometric data is handled entirely by Apple's Secure Enclave and is never accessible to us.

No data is sent to any third-party server.

Currency exchange rates

If you log expenses in a foreign currency, the app may fetch exchange rates from a public rates API. This request contains no personal information — only the currency codes you query.

Children's privacy

Ledger Lite does not knowingly collect any information from anyone, including children under 13.

Changes to this policy

If we ever change this policy, the updated version will be posted at this URL with a revised date.
